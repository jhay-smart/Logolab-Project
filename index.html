Who We Are

Our Story

Logalab.ng was born from real experiences in rural communities where basic amenities like healthcare, education, and sanitation are often missing. These gaps showed us how vital and how close local government really is.

In 2023, while volunteering with the Youth Initiative for Good Governance and Development in Bayelsa State, we heard widespread distrust in government. We turned that frustration into action: building tools for accountability and better governance.

Vision

To become Nigeria‚Äôs leading open, accessible, interactive, and simplified hub for local government data, empowering rural communities to drive accountability, transparency, and grassroots development through active civic participation.

Mission & One-Liner

To simplify complex local government data and make it accessible and understandable for rural citizens empowering them to demand accountability, transparency, and better governance while building a community of active, informed youth committed to local change.

Logalab Vote Smart: How Data Helps Us Choose Better Leaders in Nigeria ‚Äì UM6P Outreach Recap

Posted on February 2, 2026 | Rabat, Morocco

On February 1, 2026, the logalab.ng team proudly launched our "Vote Smart" initiative with a dynamic outreach event at Universit√© Mohammed VI Polytechnique (UM6P) in Morocco. This student-led effort targeted the vibrant Nigerian community on campus, aiming to spark meaningful conversations about the power of accessible data in transforming local governance and electoral choices back home in Nigeria.

Nigeria's local government areas (LGAs) receive billions in monthly allocations from the federal government, yet many communities continue to face persistent challenges poor roads, unreliable electricity, inadequate schools, health services, and security issues. As the event emphasized: the problem is not a lack of money, it's a lack of transparency and accountability. Without citizens having clear, understandable access to how funds are allocated, spent, and implemented in projects, leaders cannot be held truly responsible, and development stalls at the grassroots level.

The UM6P event was designed as an interactive, hands-on simulation rather than a conventional lecture. Participants stepped into the roles of Citizens, Candidates, and Election Officials in a simulated Local Government Area (LGA) modeled after their own class or group. The "Student Representative" candidates were vying to control key decisions: events and budget allocations, student welfare initiatives, and academic/social projects.

Through multiple voting rounds, participants experienced firsthand how data transparency influences electoral outcomes. Citizens used simplified data visualizations to assess candidates' proposed budgets and project plans. They voted based on who demonstrated the best understanding of community needs and fiscal responsibility.
