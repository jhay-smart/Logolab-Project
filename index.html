Who We Are

Local Government Accountability Lab Nigeria (logalab.ng) was born from grassroots experiences in rural communities, where limited access to basic amenities revealed the critical role of local governance.

Inspired by volunteer work in 2023 with the Youth Initiative for Good Governance and Development in Bayelsa State, we discovered widespread distrust in government. We are channeling that energy into constructive accountability to drive better governance.

Vision: To be Nigeria’s leading open, accessible, interactive, and simplified local government data hub supporting rural efforts for accountability, transparency, and grassroots development.

One-Liner: Simplifying data for local accountability; Accountability for development.

Outreach Activity at UM6P: Sensitizing Nigerians on Their Right to Vote

Posted on February 2, 2026

On February 2, 2026, the logalab.ng team organized a successful outreach event at Université Mohammed VI Polytechnique (UM6P) in Morocco. We gathered Nigerians from the community to discuss the fundamental right to vote and its crucial importance in shaping good governance and grassroots development.

During the event, we educated participants on how voting empowers citizens to hold leaders accountable, influences local policies, and drives positive change in their communities back home. We shared stories from our past initiatives, like the 2023 PVC registration drive in Bayelsa State, where we reached over 10,000 people and helped 2,000 young adults register to vote for the first time.

Activities included interactive sessions, Q&A panels, and group discussions on overcoming voter apathy and the role of technology in civic engagement. We also distributed informational materials and encouraged attendees to become Rural Accountability Agents in their local government areas.
